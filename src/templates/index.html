Aim: Model and predict the distribution of extreme temperatures and maximum precipitation in the USA.

Spatiotemporal Modelling of US Climate Change


Temporal Forecasting

We use \( \mbox{AR}(p), p^{th} \) order autoregressive time series models to model changes. Check out our models by clicking on the link above!

Spatial Forecasting

For spatial prediction we opted for a Bayesian Additive Regression Tree (BART), where we model a sum of trees model \( y(\cdot) = \sum_i f(\cdot;T_j,M_j) + \epsilon. \)
(Chipman et al., 2018). To implement this, we use Markov chain Monte Carlo to sample from the posterior and obtained a posterior predictive mean using Monte Carlo integration.

Spatiotemporal Forecasting!

Spatiotemporal modelling is a relatively new field of statistical study, where we are not only interested in the evolution of time or space as a separate entity, but together as a bivariate covariate. To do this, we use [Gaussian processes](http://www.gaussianprocess.org/gpml/chapters/RW.pdf) with the Gaussian kernel function. There are of course more suitable kernels that we could have explored if time permitting, but this provides a proof of concept as to deploying academic research topics to "simple-to-understand" web applications.
